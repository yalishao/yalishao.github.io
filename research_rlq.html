Radio-loud Quasars at z > 4

Millions of Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) have been found by now over a very wide redshift range from the local Universe to z = 7.5. Among them, only ~ 10% are classified as radio-loud sources. The origin and evolution of radio emission is one of the biggest issues in the study of AGN. The onset of radio activity is often thought to be related to merger or accretion events in the host galaxy, which provide enough fuel to feed the central AGN. As a consequence, the radio emission evolves in a dense and possibly inhomogeneous ambient medium which can influence black hole growth at least in its early stages. The ideal targets to understand such problem are the young radio sources, whose radio lobes still reside within the innermost region of the host galaxy.

We have conducted low- and high-frequency radio continuum observations toward radio-loud quasars at z > 5, including a GMRT 323 MHz project (Shao et al. 2020), and VLA S-, C- and X-band project. Six radio-loud quasars have radio spectral turnover above 5 GHz in the rest frame, making them HFPs. To better constrain the low-frequency part radio spectra, we have further uGMRT 235 and 610 MHz project. The origin of the spectral turnover can be investigated through the magnetic field and the spectral age. The key is to accurately measure the peak frequency and the source size at the turnover frequency.
